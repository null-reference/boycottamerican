Understanding the Trade Situation

$400B+

Annual bilateral trade affected

$85B

Canadian exports at risk

1.7M

Canadian jobs supported by U.S. trade

The recent trade restrictions imposed by the United States against Canada represent an unprecedented challenge to our long-standing economic partnership. As our nation's largest trading partner takes this adversarial stance, Canadians are responding with unity and determination to protect our economy and workers.

This movement is not about animosity toward American citizens but rather a reasoned economic response to protect Canadian interests in the face of unexpected trade barriers.

Affected Canadian Industries

Forestry Products

Lumber, paper, and wood products face new tariffs and restrictions despite previous agreements.

Agricultural Goods

Dairy, meat, grains, and other farm products are seeing increased restrictions at the border.

Energy Resources

Oil, natural gas, and electricity exports are facing new regulatory hurdles and limitations.
